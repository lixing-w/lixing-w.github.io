Music

Here is a collection of my music. I love exploring electronic sounds and experimenting with harmonies heavily influenced by Japanese music and jazz. Take a listen whenever you feel comfortable.

KONOHA' Sakurai · Telescope

UFO

A calm, evocative song. I experimented with processing guitar recordings to create the background sound effects. To create an illusive effect, I layered three pianos, each with slightly randomized tuning and processed with saturators and Serum FX. A guitar arpeggiates throughout, and I carefully applied reverb and delay effects to render the music while maintaining the clarity of every element. Extensive foley sounds were added to enhance the details of the song. Written in September 2024.

After School

A casual and refreshing song featuring extensive foley, humorous synth design, abundant melodic elements, and modulations that take flight.

For the sound design part, I applied automation to the resonance filter to create the "wawa" sound and added a spike of square wave to create a clean attack of the lead. I also explored using mute notes on the guitars to add a funky and humorous vibe. I experimented with frequent modulations between Ab major and Cb major in the second section to create a unique feel, and many tiny foley sounds were adopted to make the track more delicate. Written in August 2024.

STELLA LOVE

An intense, upbeat electronic track with heavy experimentation on arpeggiators and distortion, vocal chops.

I layered two arps and each of them took different roles. One is downsampled and creates a glitched feeling, and the other has extended reverb that renders the soundscape. For the vocal part, I used SynthV, an AI engine, to generate vocal phrases and then applied processings to create the chops. Written in July 2024.

Antenna

A calm, warm track featuring a looping guitar phrase, smooth pads, vocal elements, sus chords, and slightly heightened tuning, depicting a tranquil summer night.

I carefully humanized MIDI for the guitars by adding mutes, very short notes, and rubato in solo sections. To create an illusory feeling, I applied automation to randomizing the tuning of the piano, and used arpeggiator and pan automation for synths. Background noises were made by modulating saw waves and randomizing the pitch, simulating radio noises. I also simulated the sound of railroad crossing bell by playing block chords on a piano. Written in July 2024.

Wandering Wings

An impressionism piano solo written in my senior year of high school when I was studying Ravel's approach to music. Written entirely in MIDI (YES this is ALL MIDI!), the piece depicts the imagery of birds through fast arpeggios. Written in August 2023.
